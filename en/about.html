WHY IT EXISTS

Stop Paying
Per Minute.

Sbobinator exists because most people don't need a dev tool — they need text from a recording. Cloud transcribers charge by the minute, keep copies of your audio, and disappear when you cancel. We built something you install once on your own PC: upload a meeting, get a document back, keep your privacy.

Evolution

From Demo to Operator Tool

2024

The One-Shot Era

Early prototypes: drag a file, wait, get text. Fine for a five-minute clip — useless for a two-hour board meeting or a folder of podcast episodes. No history, no queue, no recovery after a crash.

THE PIVOT
2025-2026

Engine + Queue

Switched to NVIDIA NeMo Parakeet for Italian-first ASR. Added a background worker, SQLite job store, live progress in the web UI, and chunked transcription for long recordings. Transcription stays local; summaries become strictly opt-in.

2026-Present

Deployable Stack

Docker Compose for Linux mini PCs, bilingual MkDocs, license clarity for personal vs commercial use, and a growing roadmap (multilingual ASR, desktop shell). Built for people who transcribe every week — not once a year.

Philosophy

Local First. Operator Always.

Your Audio Never Leaves for ASR Parakeet runs on your CPU or GPU. Models are downloaded once and cached under your data directory. No subscription, no upload queue, no vendor lock-in for the core transcription step.
Jobs That Survive Reality Upload multiple files, close the browser, reboot the machine. The worker picks up queued jobs, recovers orphaned runs, and reconciles disk artifacts with the database — because real operators do not babysit a progress bar.
LLM Only When You Opt In Summaries are a separate layer: cloud APIs with your keys, or local Qwen for offline pipelines. Transcription quality does not depend on any external service.

Privacy Protocol

Data Stays Yours

Sbobinator treats sensitive audio like credentials: default-deny for the cloud, explicit consent for anything that leaves the machine.

On-Disk Residency

Source files, WAV extracts, transcripts, SRT cues, and optional summaries live under your configured data directory. The web UI reads and serves them locally — nothing is mirrored to a SaaS backend for transcription.

ASR path: 100% local

LLM Opt-In Gate

Cloud summary providers only run after you configure API keys in /settings/summary. Local Qwen requires a separate model download. If summary is disabled, the pipeline ends at TXT and SRT — full stop.

Summary layer: operator-controlled
