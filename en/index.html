01. Upload and leave

Upload the file, close your laptop, come back to text

Most people postpone transcription because they expect a fragile setup and a complicated workflow. This path stays simple: install, upload, wait, download. If your first concern is "Will this run on my PC?" you will find clear minimums in the quickstart and immediate feedback once you launch. If your second concern is "Will Italian quality be usable?" the fastest proof is your own file, in your own voice context, tonight.

01

Upload

Start with one file you already know: a client call, an episode draft, an interview, or a lecture capture. Marco likes to begin with yesterday's meeting because he can compare the output against his own notes in minutes. Sara often tests with a 20-minute lecture segment before pushing full recordings. That first upload removes install fear faster than any feature list.

From browser, no account gate
02

Wait (or walk away)

Giulia usually queues an episode, goes back to editing thumbnails, and checks results later. Luca often drops a long interview and moves directly to drafting his headline ideas while the file runs. You do not need to stare at a progress bar, and long recordings are expected behavior, not an edge case. The workflow is designed for people with real deadlines and multiple tasks open.

Progress check when you return
03

Transcribe

The output is built for spoken Italian: overlapping voices, informal pace, and imperfect audio that real meetings create. Luca uses transcripts to quote faster, then listens only where precision matters. Sara searches exact terms from class and jumps to sections that need review. The goal is not theoretical perfection; the goal is reducing hours of manual replay.

Italian-first output for real speech
04

Download

You receive files you can use immediately: readable text for notes, search, and quoting, plus subtitles for publishing workflows. Marco forwards action points faster, Giulia ships captions faster, and Sara builds study summaries faster. If you want additional recap features, they stay optional and secondary. Your core win is simple: one recording becomes usable writing.
